# Meaning in a Post-Agent World

The AI era brings a paradox for builders: as tools become more powerful, the joy of building can feel diminished. This article explores reclaiming agency and purpose when the process itself matters as much as the output.

## The Builder's Slump

For those who love building—not just shipping, but the act of creation itself—the current moment can feel disorienting. The world's message is clear: your thinking process is extraneous, unnecessary, and must be commoditized and compressed.

But humans are multidimensional. We need room to touch the code, to explore, to rise above local minima. In engineering, the journey is the destination. The working system in production is our reward, but we always move toward that through a process that matters.

## Reclaiming Agency

The antidote to the builder's slump is deliberate inefficiency—activities that reconnect you with the craft:

**Build from scratch**
- Create something with your bare hands and your brain
- No AI, no frameworks—just fundamentals
- Create a private repo and merge to main without a PR

**Engage with foundations**
- Read programming books written on dead trees
- Reason through something on the back of an envelope
- Scribble and daydream
- Pick apart the transformer model down to the attention mechanism and RoPE embeddings
- Explain it back to yourself

**Connect with community**
- Join Discords where people are actively building and reviewing pull requests
- Write a blog post
- Mentor a colleague
- Answer an email from a budding engineer
- Go to an in-person meetup

**Explore under the hood**
- Take a library or tool and look underneath
- Understand how it works, not just how to use it

## The Gift of Inefficiency

These activities aren't the most efficient path to shipping software. That's the point.

Having agency is one of the most important things a creator can have. The capacity to choose your own process, to engage with work in ways that feed your humanity—that hasn't been automated away. It's still there, waiting.

The builder's slump isn't a sign of obsolescence. It's a signal that something human needs attention. Give the gift of agency to yourself.
